THE DESIGN PROCESS

CONTEXT

Protohack Seattle was conducted in March 2015. Recall was the product I designed along with 4 other designers during the 1-day hackathon. The idea was iterated upon further after the hackathon and conceptualized into smartwatches.

USER RESEARCH

User Interviews

The hackthon event was a 1 day event. We spent more time in research and ideation as we had the skills to come up with quick and awesome prototypes. Sonal and Surya took the lead in coming up with a research plan. We conducted 3 interviews each for 5 minutes.

Research Questions

  • What kind of notes do they take?

  • How do they set reminders for themselves?

  • When was the last time they forgot something?

  • What kind of apps do they use to track tasks?

  • What do they put on their calendar?

  • How do they take notes and find them later?

PERSONAS

Based on our research, we identified people's problems and created personas. This quick research process helped us empathize for other people so that we didn't end up creating a product for ourselves. Note that these were only documented later for presentation, but these were the people we designed for.

TAKEAWAYS

This was a hackathon event. The main constraints were time and technology feasibility. Some of us had a programming background with which we were able to quickly determine whether some ideas were possible. This is not an industry project so it does lack the constraints that people look for in a portfolio. Nevertheless, there were a few lessons learned.

  • We converged on Material Design while we should have designed for iOS to show hand-off with the Apple Watch.

  • This was my first project involving Apple Watch. I gained a fair understanding of the Human Interface Guidelines.

  • Could have done some research before the hackathon to better inform our product design decisions.
