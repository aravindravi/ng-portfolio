PERSONAS

Our personas paved the way for an empathy driven design process. That's not in saying that documenting a persona leads to empathy, but a thoughtful and thorough research process prior to that helped gain a good understanding of the people we were designing for. This helped craft the personas in such a manner that they were instrumental in driving the decision making in the rest of the process.

SKETCHES

OVERVIEW

By the time we finished our research, we were way ahead of our budget and behind timelines. I had very little budget remaining but at the same time come up with quality wireframes and prototypes as deliverables for this phase.

STEP 1: Concept Sketching

Once we had laid out the functional and non-functional requirements, I laid out some concepts with some sketches that covered some of the key features. These were around business strategies for the clients over the actual user interface, such as features, analytics and reports.

STEP 2: UI Flow

I created some high level concepts for the user interface and rough sitemaps on the flow of screens. This was a rapid process as I could not afford to iterate much on the wireframes which was the next step. It was worth exploring quite a bit with sketches, a valuable tool indeed.

STEP 3: Mobile-First

My sketches first covered the mobile viewport. This was to not just ensure that the responsive designs were feasible, but also in making sure that the experience was consistent and intuitive across platforms. Some of the users had to access this application out on the field and it was crucial to nail this down.
