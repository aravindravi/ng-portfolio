USER RESEARCH

This project proved to be a good example of why design should be a research-driven process. About 70% of the effort was spent on research which led to numerous interesting findings.

RESEARCH METHODS

  • Interviews

    We conducted interviews with 11 participants. There were multiple rounds of interviews that helped uncover some of the problems faced by the clients. Details cannot be disclosed due to NDA. These interviews helped to gain an indepth understanding of the processes that the clients were following.

  • Survey

    We sent out surveys to select people to better understand their problems in the domain we were working on. This was a qualitative survey over a quantitative one with a small sample size. Questions were structured to get more qualitative information to keep it a more exploratory process. It helped discover the problems faced by these people in certain areas that were exploring.

  • Competitive Analysis

    Our team also conducted competitive analysis on products that existed in the market. This was a key strategy in our process as it unearthed several key insights in the domain and take the right product decisions. This also helped us make decisions on certain features of the product.

RESEARCH SUMMARY

Our process proved successful in that we were able to validate our assumptions and pivot in the right direction when required. Details cannot be disclosed.

ANALYSIS

  • Process Modeling

    From the interview data, it was clear that the process followed by the client were too complex to handle at a conceptual level. We modelled it and highlighted key areas that could be automated. This also helped converge on more specific problems.

  • Affinity Maps

    Our clients spoke about problems, needs, processes and wishlists. The wishlists were not often around their processes, but around how they could improve their business. These had to carefully categorized and having affinity maps were helpful not just for the design team, but also the clients to have a better picture of their processes.

  • Strategy

    We explored possibilities of tying into some of the existing products for the clients. We also explored any existing tools they were using for their processes to see if we could integrate with them, or design a product that would be similar to what they were used to - a decision made with the help of Personas.
