HOW I SAW THE PROBLEM

There were numerous mobile applications out there that attempted to solve this problem. Before diving into the solution, it was important to validate if this was a real problem that I was solving. (Spoiler alert: Turned out that it was). I had to consider different constraints to analyze the problem.

  • Is this a real problem and is it worth solving?

  • How can research inform the design?

  • What are competitor strengths and weaknesses?

  • What is the contextual relevance?

  • How to involve potential users in the research?

  • How can success be measured?

THE DESIGN PROCESS...

Skills involved in this project for wireframing and prototyping were quite rudimentary so I planned the process such that more research and ideation were done upfront to inform the design throughout the process.

PLANNING THE PROCESS...

Though the process has been represented linearly above, it wasn't so. There were times when ideation and sketching had to be done in parallel. Usability testing and prototyping overlapped. Ideation continuted throughout the process. Documentation of the process started on day 1 but finalized prior to presentation to stakeholders.

    Pros:

    Cons:

    Summary:

  • Out Of Milk

    Pros:

    Grocery & recipe list

    Cons:

    Unnecessary features, poor UX

    Summary:

    Fun, Meaningful, Efficient, Freemium model

Business Model

Design Considerations

  • Design for context of problem and need

  • Address needs of students and parents

  • Take users to what they need to find at the store

  • Minimize time spent on app and in store

  • Enable users to keep track of what they already have in their pantry

  • Provide offers based on search history

  • Generate shopping list based on recipes

  • Connect to an ecosystem of products that people use

  • No more barcode scanners!
