How has GDP and Infant Mortality varied in the US between 2000 - 2012

This dual graphs show that there might be a correlation between GDP and Infant Mortality Rate. The US GDP has been increasing from 2000 to 2008, while the Infant Mortality Rate remains constant. One of the potential explanation could be that the US economic at that time period was able to sustain a certain level of health care provision. There was a decrease in the Infant Mortality Rate between 2008 and 2009. That period of time also show the GDP starting to increase again. If we look at the intersection of the two lines, the changes happen almost around the year 2009. We can cross-refer that timeline to the US history, and find that President Barack Obama’s first inauguration was on January 2009. The healthcare and economic reformation could be the contributing factors to the change seen on this graph.

Which countries have maximum combined energy usage in the 10 years?

This bar graph view gives a very easy overview on who use the most energy in the world. United States and China stand out so much from the other countries. This huge difference between them and other countries have made the data from other countries to appear insignificant on the graph and hard to compare. We suggest that if the visualization is needed to understand other countries’ energy usage, it will be better to filter out data from United States and China to increase the scale of the visuals.
